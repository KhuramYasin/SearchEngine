Dr. Heather Morrison, P.E.I.'s chief public health officer, says the cases involve an individual in their 50s, an individual in their 30s, and two people in their 20s. One case is a close contact of a previously reported case, two cases are linked to a cluster in Prince County, and the other case is related to travel outside of the province. Public health on the island says all four individuals are self-isolating and contact tracing is underway. Prince Edward Island has reported 15 new cases of COVID-19 in the last four days, including a cluster of 11 cases related to Prince County, which includes outbreaks in two workplaces. The workplaces do not provide services to the public and are not considered public exposure sites.
